Air pollution, meteorological, conditions and respiratory infections in Baix Llobregat. A 14-year spatiotemporal analysis.

The objectives of this research project are:

  • Yingqing & Eli are studying all data from Paleja that is a city located in Baix Llobregat county. In this county we are using data from Air Pollution Prevention and Monitoring Network (XPCVA in catalan) corresponding to hourly data of air pollutants from year 1991-2025. Data included: nitrogen oxide (NO), mitrogen dioxid (NO2), nitrogen oxides (NOX), sulfur dioxide(SO2).
  • Meteorological data is available from Meteocat XEMA. We choose the nearest meteorological station, Vallirana. The data structure is semi-hourly from 2009, including temperature, relative humidity, wind direction, wind speed, solar irradiance.
  • Respiratory infection data are collected from Catalonia Information System for Infection Surveillance (SIVIC) using data from region 75 (Barcelona Metropolitan south), 261 is the Primary care centre code from Vallirana.

Air Pollution in Pallejà (2011–2025)

  • The graphs show how the concentrations of nitrogen oxides (NO, NO₂, NOₓ) and sulfur dioxide (SO₂) vary according to hour of the day, month of the year and day of the week. These pollutants are mainly associated with road traffic and combustion processes.

Hourly variation

  • The hourly pattern shows a strong morning peak around 7–9 h, when traffic increases during commuting hours. At this moment, NO and NO₂ rise sharply, producing the highest values of NOₓ. After this peak, concentrations decrease during midday, likely due to better atmospheric dispersion. A smaller increase appears in the evening. SO₂ remains very low and stable.

Monthly variation

  • Pollution levels show a clear seasonal cycle. Highest concentrations of nitrogen oxides occur in winter months (Dec–Feb). Concentrations decrease in spring and summer (May–Aug). Levels begin to increase again in autumn.

General interpretation and conclusion

  • Overall, road traffic is the main source of nitrogen oxide pollution in Pallejà. Seasonal differences indicate meteorological influence. Patterns show human activity and weather control pollution levels, highlighting the importance of reducing traffic emissions.
